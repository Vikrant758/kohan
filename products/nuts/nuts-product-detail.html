Nuts

Security Nuts

Security nuts, barrel nuts, security caps, and enclosures are available in a wide range of options. Our stock includes both permanent and reusable tamper-proof nuts, with special sizes available upon request.


Security nuts are designed for applications where the male mating fastener is not visible or accessible. They secure the thread to prevent theft or tampering, making them ideal for use with any bolt, screw, or stud that has a mating machine thread. While metric threads are commonly stocked, imperial threads are also available.


Our selection of anti-tamper/tamper-proof nuts includes a variety of options in stock, with other sizes and materials available for custom orders. Security barrel nuts can be provided as part of a security sex bolt set, complete with a matching male part.

Round Nut

A round nut, also known as a circular nut or dome nut, is a type of fastener with a rounded or dome-shaped top. Unlike traditional hex nuts, round nuts have a smooth, curved surface without any flats for a wrench to grip. This design often serves both aesthetic and functional purposes, such as preventing tampering or reducing the risk of snagging.


Round nuts are commonly used in applications where the appearance of the fastener is important, or where tamper resistance is desired. They can also be used in machinery and equipment to ensure that the nut doesn't catch on clothing or other materials. Some round nuts may require specialized tools for installation and removal, enhancing their security features.


Brass Insert Nut

A brass insert nut, also known as a brass threaded insert or insert nut, is a type of fastener designed to provide a durable, threaded hole in softer materials. These nuts are typically made of brass, a metal known for its corrosion resistance and machinability. They are commonly used in applications where materials like wood, plastic, or soft metals need to hold screws or bolts securely.


The main purpose of brass insert nuts is to provide a reliable threaded connection that won't strip or wear out easily. They are often installed by pressing, tapping, or heat setting into the host material. The external surface of the insert nut usually features knurls, ridges, or barbs to prevent it from rotating or pulling out once installed.


Brass insert nuts are widely used in various industries, including electronics, automotive, furniture, and construction, where they help create strong, reusable connections in assemblies.


Hex Nut

A hex nut is a type of fastener with a hexagonal (six-sided) shape. It's commonly used in conjunction with bolts, screws, or other threaded fasteners to secure two or more parts together. The hex shape allows for easy tightening and loosening with a wrench or socket, providing good grip and torque transfer.


Hex nuts are widely used in construction, automotive, machinery, and many other industries due to their versatility and ease of use.


Technial Specifications

  • Size: M5 to M30 or as per requirement.
  • Material: Carbon Steel, Alloy Steel & Stainless Steel
  • Standards: DIN 934, UNC, UNF, ISO, 4032 and ISO 4034

Heavy Hex Nut

Heavy hex nuts are a type of fastener similar in shape to standard hex nuts but with some key differences. They are larger and thicker across the flats and taller in height compared to standard hex nuts, which provides increased strength and durability. This makes them suitable for use in applications where higher strength is required.


TBecause of their robust design, heavy hex nuts are ideal for use in demanding environments, such as structural steelwork, bridges, and other large-scale infrastructure projects.


Technial Specifications

  • Standard & Specifications: IS, DIN, BS, ASTM, IS 4190, ISO, JIS, GB
  • Size Range: M3 - M48 and also available in all customized sizes.
  • Length: 3 mm to 200mm
  • Threads: Metric, BSW, BSF, UNC, UNF, and as per costumers requirement.
  • Surface Finish: Blackening, Cadmium zinc plated, Galvanized, Hot dip Galvanized, Nickel Plated, Buffing, etc.
