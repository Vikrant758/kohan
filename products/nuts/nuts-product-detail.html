Nuts

Security Nuts

Security nuts, barrel nuts, security caps, and enclosures are available in a wide range of options. Our stock includes both permanent and reusable tamper-proof nuts, with special sizes available upon request.


Security nuts are designed for applications where the male mating fastener is not visible or accessible. They secure the thread to prevent theft or tampering, making them ideal for use with any bolt, screw, or stud that has a mating machine thread. While metric threads are commonly stocked, imperial threads are also available.


Our selection of anti-tamper/tamper-proof nuts includes a variety of options in stock, with other sizes and materials available for custom orders. Security barrel nuts can be provided as part of a security sex bolt set, complete with a matching male part.

Round Nut

A round nut, also known as a circular nut or dome nut, is a type of fastener with a rounded or dome-shaped top. Unlike traditional hex nuts, round nuts have a smooth, curved surface without any flats for a wrench to grip. This design often serves both aesthetic and functional purposes, such as preventing tampering or reducing the risk of snagging.


Round nuts are commonly used in applications where the appearance of the fastener is important, or where tamper resistance is desired. They can also be used in machinery and equipment to ensure that the nut doesn't catch on clothing or other materials. Some round nuts may require specialized tools for installation and removal, enhancing their security features.


Brass Insert Nut

A brass insert nut, also known as a brass threaded insert or insert nut, is a type of fastener designed to provide a durable, threaded hole in softer materials. These nuts are typically made of brass, a metal known for its corrosion resistance and machinability. They are commonly used in applications where materials like wood, plastic, or soft metals need to hold screws or bolts securely.


The main purpose of brass insert nuts is to provide a reliable threaded connection that won't strip or wear out easily. They are often installed by pressing, tapping, or heat setting into the host material. The external surface of the insert nut usually features knurls, ridges, or barbs to prevent it from rotating or pulling out once installed.


Brass insert nuts are widely used in various industries, including electronics, automotive, furniture, and construction, where they help create strong, reusable connections in assemblies.


Hex Nut

A hex nut is a type of fastener with a hexagonal (six-sided) shape. It's commonly used in conjunction with bolts, screws, or other threaded fasteners to secure two or more parts together. The hex shape allows for easy tightening and loosening with a wrench or socket, providing good grip and torque transfer.


Hex nuts are widely used in construction, automotive, machinery, and many other industries due to their versatility and ease of use.


Technial Specifications

  • Size: M5 to M30 or as per requirement.
  • Material: Carbon Steel, Alloy Steel & Stainless Steel
  • Standards: DIN 934, UNC, UNF, ISO, 4032 and ISO 4034

Hex Slotted / Castle Nut

A hex slotted nut, also known as a castle nut or castellated nut, is a type of fastener characterized by its hexagonal shape and slots or notches cut into one end. These slots are designed to accommodate a cotter pin, which is used to secure the nut in place and prevent it from loosening due to vibration or rotation.


The term "castle nut" comes from the nut's appearance, which resembles the battlements of a castle. The primary applications of hex slotted nuts are in situations where it is critical to ensure that the nut does not come loose, such as in automotive, aerospace, and machinery applications.


Hex slotted/castle nuts are made from various materials, including steel, stainless steel, and brass, and they come in different sizes and thread pitches to suit various applications.

Technial Specifications

  • Size Range: M3 to M100
  • Standard: ASME,BS,DIN,ISO,UNI,DIN-EN
  • Usage\Application: Industrial
  • Customization is also available.

Heavy Hex Nut

Heavy hex nuts are a type of fastener similar in shape to standard hex nuts but with some key differences. They are larger and thicker across the flats and taller in height compared to standard hex nuts, which provides increased strength and durability. This makes them suitable for use in applications where higher strength is required.


Because of their robust design, heavy hex nuts are ideal for use in demanding environments, such as structural steelwork, bridges, and other large-scale infrastructure projects.


Technial Specifications

  • Standard & Specifications: IS, DIN, BS, ASTM, IS 4190, ISO, JIS, GB
  • Size Range: M3 - M48 and also available in all customized sizes.
  • Length: 3 mm to 200mm
  • Threads: Metric, BSW, BSF, UNC, UNF, and as per costumers requirement.
  • Surface Finish: Blackening, Cadmium zinc plated, Galvanized, Hot dip Galvanized, Nickel Plated, Buffing, etc.

Flange Nut

A flange nut is a type of fastener that features a wide, flat, integrated flange at one end, which acts like a washer. This design helps distribute the load over a larger area and prevents damage to the surface being secured, reducing the need for a separate washer. The flange can also help the nut stay in place and resist loosening due to vibration.


The flange nut's design simplifies the assembly process and ensures a more secure and stable fastening, making it a popular choice in many industrial and mechanical applications.


Technial Specifications

  • Size: M3 to M20
  • SS Flange Nuts Thread Direction: Right Hand.
  • Standard: DIN934 DIN936 DIN439 DIN555 A194 2H ASTM A563 JIS B1190, DIN 6923
  • Coatings: Black Oxide, Cadmium, Galvanized, Teflon, Xylan, Zinc, Others Upon Request.
  • Grade: 4,8, 10, AISI 2, AISI 5, AISI 8

Cap Nut

A cap nut, also known as a dome nut or acorn nut, is a type of fastener that has a domed top, which covers the exposed end of a bolt or threaded rod. This cap serves both functional and aesthetic purposes.


Overall, cap nuts offer both practical and visual benefits, providing protection for threaded components while enhancing the appearance of the assembled item.


Technial Specifications

  • Size: M3 to M30
  • Cap Nuts Length: 6mm to 500mm
  • Grade: 4, 8, 10, AISI 2, AISI 5, AISI 8
  • Standard: DIN934, DIN936, DIN439, DIN555, A194, 2H, ASTM A563, DIN1587

Coupling Nut

A coupling nut, also known as an extension nut, is a long, hexagonal-shaped fastener used to connect two threaded rods or bolts together. The internal threads of the coupling nut allow it to join these threaded components, effectively extending their length or facilitating the connection of different parts.


Coupling nuts are versatile and can be used in various settings, including industrial, commercial, and DIY projects. They provide a simple and effective way to extend or connect threaded components securely.


Technial Specifications

  • Sizes range from 10/32 to 5-4 internal thread, and also available in customized sizes.
  • Material: Stainless, Steel, Brass and Aluminum
  • Length: 3mm to 200 mm
  • Standard & Specifications: IS, DIN, BS, ASTM, IS 4190, ISO, JIS, GB
  • Threads: UNC, UNF, ISO, BSW & ACME

Lock Nut

A lock nut, also known as a locking nut, is a type of fastener designed to resist loosening under vibration or torque. Unlike standard nuts, lock nuts have special features that secure them in place, reducing the risk of them coming loose in applications where stability is crucial.


Lock nuts are used in various industries, including automotive, aerospace, construction, and machinery. They are essential in any application where components are subject to vibration, shifting, or where maintaining tension is critical.


Technial Specifications

  • Dimensional Standards: DIN 982 , DIN 985, ISO 7040, ISO 10511
  • SIZE RANGE: - Metric: M6 to M72- Inches: 1/4” to 1-1/2”

Square Nut

A square nut is a type of fastener with a square shape, as opposed to the more common hexagonal or round nuts. The four-sided design provides a flat, even surface that can be gripped by a wrench or tool, and it offers unique advantages in certain applications.


Square nuts are less common than hex nuts but are valuable in applications where their specific shape offers practical benefits.


Technial Specifications

  • Standard: DIN, BS, EN, IS ASTM, AMS, JIS & Other International Standards.
  • Size: M16 - M56 (Also custom sizes as per requirement).

Knurled / Thumb Nut

A knurled nut, also known as a thumb nut, is a type of fastener designed to be easily tightened or loosened by hand without the need for tools. It features a pattern of raised or textured ridges (knurling) around its circumference, which provides a better grip for manual operation.


Square nuts are less common than hex nuts but are valuable in applications where their specific shape offers practical benefits.

  • Standard: ASTM, ASME, DIN, BS, EN, IS, AMS, JIS, IBR & Other International Standards.
  • Size: M02 to M33 (as per customer requirement)
  • Length: 3 mm to 200 mm
